Hindrances toward solving the problem

This problem seem to be rising as the time goes on and prove to be hard to diminish as a result of various factors including the following;

  1. Poverty: in fact, most of the coastal dwellers in Paje and Tumbatu Island depend on fishing activity for their living. This is due to lack of permanent alternative source of employment which forces them to over depend on fishing for food as well as for their general basic needs. Therefore, this exposes the coral reefs to be in danger as they are maximally consumed.
  2. Lack of reasonable researches (mapping) and follow up: a greater percentage of coral reefs in Paje and Tumbatu Island is not mapped, also there is no enough researches conducted concerning the location and condition of the coral reefs which acts as an obstacle towards monitoring of coral reefs.
  3. Improper implementation of policies and laws: various conservation, and protective policies and laws have been formulated but still there is no improper implementation of these policies. For example, fisheries law No. 22 of 2003 which handles the authority to the fisheries officers to imprison the defendant who are caught guilty, but in fact they are not caught and if caught there is no any further procedure to be taken.
  4. Low awareness of the local community people on the ecological importance of coral reefs in the marine ecosystem: local fishermen do understand that coral reefs are the habitat but they lack enough knowledge and understanding on the ecological knowledge concerning the role played by the coral reefs towards other marine species which triggers the destruction of coral reefs.
